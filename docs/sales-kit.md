# sales-kit.md: Sales Kit

For selling Plato to restaurant owners on Aruba. Use the one-pager to open, the demo to sell, and the objection answers to close.

## 1. The one-pager

Headline: People eat with their eyes. Your menu should move.

What it is: Plato turns your menu into a fast page of short food videos that diners open by scanning a QR at the table. No app, no download. It loads in seconds on any phone, in English and Spanish, with prices in dollars or florins.

Why it matters on Aruba:
- Tourists spent over 200 million dollars on dining here last year. They decide with their phones.
- A video of the dish sells better than a line of text and a price.
- You look modern next to the place next door with a paper menu.

What you get:
- A filmed video menu we shoot and build for you, on site, done for you.
- Your own link, your QR stand, stickers, and a window decal.
- A simple dashboard to mark a dish sold out or change a price in one tap.
- Analytics that show your most viewed dishes and how many people scanned.

What it costs: plans from 99 dollars a month, with a one-time setup. We do the filming and the build. You approve and go live, usually within a day or two of the shoot.

Call to action: Let us film three of your dishes free and show you the page on your phone. If you like it, we go live this week.

## 2. The demo script

Goal: get to the phone moment fast. The first time an owner sees their own food moving on a clean page, that is the sale.

1. Open with the problem. Ask, how do tourists pick your dishes right now. Listen. Most say paper menu or a board.
2. Show a live Plato menu on your phone. Scan the QR yourself so they see the speed. Scroll the videos. Say nothing for a few seconds and let the food do the work.
3. Tie it to them. Point at one of their dishes and say, imagine this one moving, with the price, in English and Spanish, one scan away.
4. Show the dashboard. Mark a dish sold out, change a price. Say, that is all you do. We handle the rest.
5. Show the QR stand and decal. Say, we place these, and every scan is counted.
6. Make the offer. We film three dishes free this week and show you the page. If you like it, we build the full menu and go live.

Keep it under ten minutes. The phone does the selling.

## 3. Objections and answers

We already have a QR menu that opens a PDF.
- A PDF is a paper menu on a screen. It is slow and it does not sell. Plato is fast and the food moves. Diners order what they can see.

I do not have time for this.
- That is the point. We film and build it for you on site. You give us a couple of hours once, then a tap when something sells out.

It is too expensive.
- One extra order a day covers the plan. The dishes that look best on video are usually your high-margin plates. We feature those.

My food will not look good on video.
- We shoot it for you to a set standard, good light, the dish filling the frame, a simple motion. It looks better than you expect. Let us prove it with three free dishes.

What if I want to change something.
- Sold out and prices are one tap in your dashboard. Anything bigger, you send a request and we handle it, usually within two business days.

I am worried about a contract.
- Plans are flexible. The setup fee is one time. You can see the value before you commit, with the free three-dish demo.

Tourists do not speak Spanish, why both languages.
- Most of your visitors read English, and many locals and South American guests prefer Spanish. The toggle covers both, and Dutch and Papiamento are coming.

## 4. Target list to start
- Casual and mid-range restaurants with foot traffic in tourist areas.
- Places with strong-looking signature dishes.
- Food trucks and beach spots where a phone menu fits the setting.
- Owners you already know through the island network. Warm intros close faster.
