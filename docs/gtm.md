# gtm.md: Go To Market, Sales, Physical Products

## 1. Positioning
"Video menus that turn tourists into diners." Built in Aruba, for Aruba. The owner gets a beautiful page, the tools to drive scans, and a local partner who films the food.

Lead with the diner insight: visitors decide what to eat from video and photos before they sit down. A text menu loses to a video menu every time.

## 2. The wedge: a free flagship
Pick one well-known, well-located restaurant. Build its video menu free. Film it. Make it stunning. This becomes the demo you show every other owner. One great reference sells the next ten. Choose a spot with heavy tourist traffic and an owner who talks to peers.

## 3. Sales motion
1. Walk in during slow hours. Show the flagship page on your phone.
2. Offer to build their first three dishes free on the spot or within a day.
3. Send them the live link. Let them feel the pride of seeing their food move.
4. Close on a plan. Offer annual with two months free.
5. Place hardware that week. QR on tables, NFC card at the host stand, window decal at the door.
6. Ask for one referral. Restaurant owners know other owners.

Zones to work in order: Palm Beach high-rise strip, Eagle Beach low-rise, Oranjestad downtown and the marina, Noord, then San Nicolas.

## 4. Physical product layer

This is the bridge from phone to table and the part rivals find hard to copy at distance.

- QR stickers. Vinyl, on tables and the bar. Restaurant name plus the QR. Scan goes to the menu page.
- NFC stickers and cards. NTAG215. A diner taps the phone and the page opens. No camera needed. Place at the host stand and on tables.
- Table stands. Acrylic, reusable, holds the QR upright. Looks premium.
- Window decal. "Scan to see our menu" at the door. Catches passersby who decide where to eat.
- Flyers. Designed in Canva with the QR. For takeout bags, hotel concierge desks, tour operators.
- Tablet display, rented. A tablet on the counter or entrance in kiosk mode, looping the video menu. You own it, lock it to their page, and reclaim it if they leave. Optional add-on on any plan. See finance.md section 3a.

Sourcing:
- NFC NTAG215 stickers and cards from bulk suppliers online. Buy in lots of 100.
- Vinyl QR stickers and window decals from a local print shop or an online printer. Local keeps reorders fast.
- Acrylic stands wholesale, branded with a small Plato mark and the restaurant name.
- Tablets: entry iPad or a refurbished unit, or a good Android tablet. Start with one or two to test demand. Lock each to kiosk mode pinned to the menu URL.
- Keep a small stock so you can place hardware the same week you close.

Fulfillment: manual at first through the order queue in admin. Track the rented tablet fleet in the admin Tablets view.

## 5. Pricing the physical layer
Hardware is tied to the plan, since content capture and a stand come with every plan.
- Starter includes on-site capture, the live menu, and one QR code stand.
- Growth adds the NFC and QR sticker pack and a window decal.
- Premium adds the full hardware kit and flyer design.
- Tablet display is an optional rental on any plan, about $35 per month, with a small deposit and a minimum term.
- Extra hardware sells at cost plus a fair margin. Never give away the premium feel for free.

## 6. Partnerships and channels
- Hotels and resorts. Concierge desks recommend restaurants. A flyer or QR card in the room or at the desk drives scans. Offer hotels a simple co-marketing tie in.
- Tourism authority and AHATA. Position as a local digital tool that improves the visitor experience and supports small operators. Fits the regenerative and digital agenda.
- Food trucks and beach bars. Lower price point, high volume, fast to onboard. A whole segment on Starter.
- Tour and excursion operators. They send guests to eat. A directory link helps.
- Your own network. Use your government and community relationships to open doors and earn trust.

## 7. Content and proof
- Post before and after clips of menus you build. The transformation sells.
- Film short owner testimonials. Video proof for a video product.
- Show the analytics wins. "This dish got 400 video plays in a week and sales rose."
- Run the faith and builder content style you already use to tell the founder story.

## 8. Retention
- Quarterly menu refresh. Seasonal items, holiday specials. Keeps the page alive and gives a reason to film again.
- Monthly analytics email. Top dishes, views, scans. Keeps the value visible.
- Respond fast. Local, same day support is a moat against foreign tools.
- The Plato Card (see section 8a). A diner who scans a QR menu is gone after one visit; a diner with the Plato Card in their wallet is an audience Plato can reach again. This is the strongest retention + lock-in lever we have.

## 8a. The Plato Card — coalition loyalty + a paid channel

A Plato-branded Apple Wallet card diners add once and present for member discounts at any partner restaurant. No plain-QR-menu competitor can copy it, because they do not own a diner audience.

- Positioning: "Your video menu + a card diners keep, and specials that land on their lock screen." Turns a one-time scan into a channel.
- For restaurants: list a member perk to get foot traffic, and buy a promoted blast to reach every Plato member island-wide. Only Plato sends, so quality stays high.
- Revenue: $75/blast or $199/mo bundle; Premium gets 1 free/mo. See finance.md section 10.
- The network effect: every new partner restaurant grows the member base, which makes a blast worth more, which makes partnering more attractive. Plato becomes the hub.
- v1 is the broadcast/discount card. Per-diner accumulating points (scan to earn) is a later phase if traction warrants. Apple Wallet only for now; Android later.

## 9. Expansion path
- Win Aruba first. Aim for 40 in year 1, 90 in year 2.
- Add Curacao and Bonaire. Same language zone, same playbook.
- Consider licensing the platform to a local operator on each island rather than running everything yourself.
- Open the Discovery directory once enough restaurants are live to make it a real visitor tool. Then charge for featured placement.

## 10. First 10 customers checklist
- 1 free flagship, filmed and stunning.
- 3 paying in tourist zones within 30 days of launch.
- 1 hotel conversation started.
- 1 food truck or beach bar on Starter to prove the low tier.
- 5 testimonials or before and after clips captured.
- A simple one page price sheet and a demo link ready to send.
